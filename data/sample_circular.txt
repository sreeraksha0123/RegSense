Clause 4.2 — Client KYC Re-verification
Stockbrokers shall re-verify the Know Your Customer (KYC) records of all
active clients at least once every two years, and shall update any change
in client information (address, bank account, income range, etc.) within
seven working days of becoming aware of such change. Stockbrokers must
maintain documentary proof of each re-verification cycle for inspection
by the exchange or SEBI.

Clause 7.1 — Daily Margin Reporting
Every stockbroker shall report client-wise margin collected (cash, cash
equivalent, and non-cash) to the relevant stock exchange on a daily basis,
no later than the end of the next trading day (T+1). Reports must
reconcile against the broker's internal ledger and any discrepancy
exceeding 1% must be flagged and explained in the same submission.

Clause 9.3 — Segregation of Client Funds
Stockbrokers shall maintain client funds in a separate "Client Bank
Account," distinct from the broker's own funds, at all times. Commingling
of client and proprietary funds, even temporarily, is prohibited.
Brokers shall reconcile the Client Bank Account balance against client
ledger balances on a daily basis and retain reconciliation records for a
minimum of five years.
